# Sapore è Sapere — Vision & Voice Playbook

This skill defines **how the website should feel, sound, and behave** — the
strategic and editorial layer on top of the raw facts. Where
[restaurant-info](../restaurant-info/SKILL.md) answers *"what is true?"*, this skill
answers *"who are we, what do we want the visitor to feel, and how do we say it?"*

Read this before writing any headline, paragraph, button label, or page layout.

## The idea in one line

> **Sapore è Sapere** — *il sapore nasce dal sapere.*
> Flavor born from knowledge: stellar technique made warm, personal, and creative.

The name is a play on words (*sapore* = flavor, *sapere* = knowledge). It is the
spine of the whole brand: every dish is the result of years of learning in
Michelin-starred and luxury kitchens, translated into bold, original, accessible
hospitality. Lean on this idea constantly — it is the restaurant's single most
distinctive asset.

## Identity at a glance

- **Essence:** Knowledge you can taste — creativity grounded in real technique.
- **Cuisine:** Creative seafood, vegetarian options, inventive pairings.
- **Personality:** Refined but warm, confident but never pretentious, curious, generous.
- **Promise:** "Un'esperienza gastronomica fuori dal comune" delivered without stiffness.
- **Pedigree:** Chef Marco Taglione (Colonna ⭐, Cannavacciuolo ⭐, Jumeirah, Boscolo) + pastry chef / maître Marika Urbani.
- **Place & since:** Frosinone (FR), Italy — since 23 May 2019.

## How to use this skill (quick rules)

1. Website **content is in Italian**; this playbook's framing is in English.
2. Never claim awards, dishes, or facts not present in `restaurant-info`. Creativity lives in *expression*, not invented facts.
3. Every page should make the *sapore è sapere* idea felt at least once — explicitly or through the writing.
4. Keep it elegant and warm. If a sentence sounds like a corporate brochure or a hype ad, rewrite it.
5. Performance and clarity are part of the brand: pure HTML/CSS, fast, accessible, uncluttered.
