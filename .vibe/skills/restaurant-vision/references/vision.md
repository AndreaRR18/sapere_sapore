# Website Vision — Sapore è Sapere

## Purpose

The website exists to **turn curiosity into a reservation**. It is the digital
front door of the restaurant: it must convey, in seconds, that this is a creative
seafood restaurant of unusual quality — starred-kitchen technique in a warm,
elegant setting — and make it effortless to see the menu, understand the experience,
and get in touch.

It is a **brochure/showcase site**, not an e-commerce platform. Success is measured in
calls, emails, map visits, and remembered impressions — not transactions.

## Vision statement

> A fast, beautiful, unmistakably *Sapore è Sapere* website that makes a first-time
> visitor think "I want to eat here" and gives a returning guest everything they need
> in one tap. Every page should feel like the restaurant: refined, warm, creative,
> and considered down to the last detail.

## Primary audiences

1. **The local diner / couple** looking for somewhere special tonight or this weekend.
   Needs: menu, vibe, hours, location, phone — fast, especially on mobile.
2. **The occasion planner** (anniversary, family gathering, group, corporate dinner).
   Needs: reassurance it's the right setting, sense of range, easy contact.
3. **The food-curious / out-of-towner** discovering the chef's story and creativity.
   Needs: the narrative, the photos, the pedigree — reasons to make the trip.

## What every page must achieve

| Page          | Job to be done | Primary CTA |
|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Home**      | Communicate identity + the *sapore è sapere* idea instantly; route visitors. | Vedi il menù / Prenota |
| **Menù**      | Show the creativity and value; make dishes feel desirable; tasting menu front and centre. | Prenota un tavolo |
| **Vini**      | Convey the 100+ label depth and the pairing experience. | Prenota / Vedi menù |
| **Chef**      | Earn trust through the chef's pedigree and the couple's story; make it human. | Scopri il menù |
| **Contatti**  | Remove all friction to visiting: address, map, phone, email, hours. | Chiama / Apri in mappa |

## Experience principles

- **Mobile-first.** Most visitors arrive on a phone, often deciding where to eat *now*.
  Phone number and hours must be reachable in one or two taps from any page.
- **Speed is hospitality.** Pure HTML/CSS, optimized images, no blocking scripts.
  A slow site contradicts the brand promise of care.
- **Show, then tell.** Lead with strong imagery and a clear line; support with concise copy.
- **One clear action per screen.** Never make the visitor wonder what to do next.
- **Accessible by default.** Readable contrast, semantic markup, keyboard-friendly nav,
  reduced-motion support, alt text on every image.
- **Trust signals woven in.** Starred-kitchen background, awards/certificates, allergen
  and HACCP diligence — presented as care, not bragging.

## Success criteria

- A first-time visitor can describe what kind of restaurant this is within ~5 seconds.
- Menu, hours, and contact are each reachable in ≤ 2 taps from the home page.
- The site loads fast on mobile (lightweight pages, optimized images, no JS runtime).
- Copy and visuals feel consistently "Sapore è Sapere" across all five pages.
- Clear, frictionless paths to call, email, or open the location in maps.

## Explicit non-goals (for now)

- No online booking/payment system, no account logins, no blog/CMS.
- No JavaScript framework, no heavy libraries — keep it static and fast.
- No invented content: claims and facts come only from `restaurant-info`.
- These can be revisited later (see Phase 3 in [website-plan.md](website-plan.md)).
